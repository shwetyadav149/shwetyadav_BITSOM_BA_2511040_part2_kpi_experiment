# Recommendation Memo

## Executive Summary

A controlled experiment was conducted to evaluate whether a new onboarding and activation campaign improved customer conversion and engagement outcomes.

The treatment group outperformed the control group on the primary success metric and several supporting metrics.

---

## Business Objective

Determine whether the new onboarding experience should be rolled out to all users.

---

## North Star Metric

Paid Conversion Rate

The selected North Star Metric directly measures the ability of the onboarding process to create paying customers.

---

## KPI Tree Summary

The KPI framework focused on three primary business drivers:

* Acquisition
* Activation
* Monetization

Guardrail metrics were monitored to ensure that improvements in conversion quality did not negatively impact customer experience.

---

## Experiment Results

| Metric                  | Control | Treatment |
| ----------------------- | ------- | --------- |
| Paid Conversion Rate    | 3.17%   | 6.99%     |
| Engagement Score        | 57.03   | 62.93     |
| Average Days to Convert | 8.86    | 6.40      |
| Refund Rate             | 0.00%   | 0.42%     |
| Support Ticket Rate     | 14.72%  | 24.76%    |

---

## Hypothesis Test Interpretation

The experiment produced a P-Value of 0.00057 which is below the significance threshold of 0.05.

The increase in paid conversion rate is statistically significant.

---

## Guardrail Analysis

While conversion performance improved substantially, support ticket rates increased significantly.

This suggests that some users may require additional assistance during onboarding.

Refund rates increased only slightly and engagement scores improved.

---

## Segment Insights

The treatment generally performed better across regions, devices, and traffic sources.

The strongest gains were observed among mobile users and email-acquired customers.

---

## Final Recommendation

Launch the treatment gradually through a phased rollout strategy while closely monitoring customer support volume.

A full rollout should occur only after support issues are investigated and onboarding friction is reduced.

---

## Risks and Limitations

* Short experiment duration
* Potential seasonal effects
* Limited visibility into long-term retention behavior

---

## Next Steps

1. Investigate causes of increased support tickets.
2. Monitor customer retention and churn.
3. Conduct follow-up experiments on onboarding improvements.
4. Evaluate customer lifetime value after rollout.
